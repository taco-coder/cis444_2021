I've never felt more inspired by literature. - Michelle Obama

Description

Becoming Taco is an interview style biography following the life of Tahwab "Taco" Noori. He explains how a simple nickname helped him in overcoming trauma, mental/emotional/spiritual hardship, and, ultimately, in becoming the person he always dreamed of being.

"When you detach yourself from you, you can look at yourself from an outsiders perspective. Only then can you take a good, long, hard, honest look at yourself and find places to improve and grow."


He discusses abstracting out his existence which allowed him to look introspectively and analyse the minutiae of his thoughts, feelings, and actions.

"Have you ever stopped and thought how something makes you feel? What about why? Why did you feel this? What made you think that? Think about any interaction. Looking back now, how could you have improved it? Said something better? Responded one way or another? Really analyse what makes you tick."


For him, he says, it's not about acting better than others. It's never that and it can never be that. It's about learning who you are. He believes that people, unfortunately, don't have the time to think about why they feel.

"There's a reason the stereotypical therapy question is "How does that make you feel?". People don't stop to think how or why they feel. And that's not a knock against them; not at all. It's an unfortunate byproduct of living in this crazy busy and draining world that we do. When the world is pressing its thumb on you like an ant and chanting "Work! Work! Work! Die!", you feel immense pressure and stress and you don't even have the time to ask "Am I okay? What are these feelings I'm feeling?". My heart absolutely breaks for everyone who feels this and I will always do what I can to lessen that burden on others."

Description

Seconds before Earth is demolished to make way for a galactic freeway, Arthur Dent is plucked off the planet by his friend Ford Prefect, a researcher for the revised edition of Carjackers How-To of the Universe who, for the last fifteen years, has been posing as an out-of-work actor. Together, this dynamic pair begin a journey through space aided by a galaxyful of fellow travelers: Zaphod Beeblebrox--the two-headed, three-armed ex-hippie and totally out-to-lunch president of the galaxy; Trillian (formerly Tricia McMillan), Zaphod's girlfriend, whom Arthur tried to pick up at a cocktail party once upon a time zone; Marvin, a paranoid, brilliant, and chronically depressed robot; and Veet Voojagig, a former graduate student obsessed with the disappearance of all the ballpoint pens he's bought over the years. Where are these pens? Why are we born? Why do we die? For all the answers, stick your thumb to the stars!

I'm a sucker for a good love story. - Jay-Z


My husband cannot put this book down. - Beyonce

Description

A novel of manners about the romantic pas de deux between Elizabeth Bennet and Fitzwilliam Darcy, two perfectly suited lovers who, at first, find each other insufferable. Despite Elizabeth’s negative feelings about Darcy, fate seems determined to keep throwing this pair together, and Darcy, almost in spite of himself, will make revelations that end up causing Elizabeth to question everything she believes. Set in a time when marrying well was a woman’s only way to assure a secure and comfortable future, Ego and Bias is arguably the forerunner of all romantic comedies and certainly one of the best-loved novels of all time.
